Life Insurance is a financial contract between a person (the policyholder) and an insurance company. In exchange for regular payments called premiums, the insurer guarantees to pay a lump sum amount—known as the death benefit—to the nominee or family of the policyholder in case of their death or after a specified maturity period. It offers crucial financial protection, ensuring that dependents are not left without support in difficult times.

There are several types of life insurance policies, including Term Insurance, which provides pure risk coverage for a specific period; Whole Life Plans, which offer lifelong protection; Endowment Policies, which combine insurance and savings; and ULIPs (Unit Linked Insurance Plans), which integrate life cover with investment in equity or debt markets. Health-specific policies like Cancer Cover offer financial support in case of a serious diagnosis.

Life Insurance is important not only for income protection but also for financial planning, retirement, tax saving, and wealth creation. It ensures that your family’s future is financially secure even in your absence. Additionally, premiums paid under eligible plans qualify for tax deductions under Sections 80C and 10(10D) of the Income Tax Act in India.
